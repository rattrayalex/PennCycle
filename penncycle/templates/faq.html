FAQ

For a membership fee, a registration process, and a swipe of PennCard, you can have full access to bikes (including locks and helmets). After you have registered, visit one of our locations (Penn Student Agencies Store, Stouffer, or Houston) and present your PennCard to an employee at the desk. Take the numbered key and find the corresponding PennCycle. Inspect the bike and report issues, adjust the seat, and PennCycle! Remember to return the bike to any location before closing!

  • Who is eligible to participate in PennCycle?

Bikes are available to the entire Penn community including students, staff, and faculty. Anyone with a PennCard.

  • How much does it cost?

Bikes are available for rental for as low as $25 a semester. Please see our Plans page for more information.

  • When are PennCycle hours/when can I rent and return bikes?

Bike rental hours vary by location. Please see our Locations page for more information on hours.

  • How are bikes assigned?

Bikes are assigned on a first-come first-serve basis.

  • For how long can I keep a rented bike?

You can keep a bike for a day. If you keep the bike overnight, overnight charges apply. However, if you have the unlimited plan, you can keep the bike out for as long as you like.

  • Drats! My bike got lost/damaged – what do I do?!

When you rent a bike, you are held responsible for its condition beyond normal wear and tear. Notify an employee at the location you rented it from of the loss or damage and email messenger@penncycle.org.

  • Who are the people in charge of PennCycle?

PennCycle is run by student managers out of Penn Student Agencies.

  • How can I pay for my PennCycle membership?
When you sign up on our website you can pay with Bursar and Credit Card online. If you would like to pay with cash, you can complete the registration process online and bring your membership fee in cash to the Penn Student Agencies Store between 10 am and 6 pm, Monday through Friday.
  • How can I get more involved in PennCycle?
We are currently looking for volunteers to publicize and help run PennCycle. Volunteers will have a high chance of promotion to management. If you are interested, or would like more information, email messenger@penncycle.org.
  • Do you have maps of bike paths in Philly?
We are currently working on finding the best bike maps. These will be up soon!

Still looking for an answer? Email messenger@penncycle.org.
